LLM-Generated Passwords Expose Major Security Flaws with Predictability, Repetition, and Weakness
New research reveals that LLM-generated passwords are significantly weaker than they seem, lacking the true randomness of cryptographically secure generators due to LLMs' predictive nature. Claude Opus 4.6 produced only 30 unique passwords across 50 runs, with one appearing 18 times (36% probability), while GPT-5.2 passwords nearly all started with "v" and Gemini 3 Flash with "K" or "k". Passwords from Claude Opus 4.6 showed only 27 bits of entropy and GPT-5.2 just 20 bits, making them vulnerable to cracking in seconds, and changing model temperature did not resolve the issue.

Large Language Model (LLM) integration risks for SaaS and enterprise
The rapid integration of LLMs into SaaS and enterprise applications is creating new security vulnerabilities, with deployments outpacing the adaptation of security models. The primary risk lies in the integration layer, where LLMs gain contextual access to sensitive data via user prompts, application context, and internal systems, creating a new trust boundary. Unlike traditional applications, LLM interfaces are open-ended and conversational, making them susceptible to prompt injection attacks that manipulate model behavior.
