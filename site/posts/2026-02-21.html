Sat, 21 Feb 2026 21:05:18 GMT · BBC News
Nasa astronauts' moon mission delayed due to rocket issue
NASA's Artemis II mission, aiming to send four astronauts on a 10-day trip around the Moon for the first time in 50 years, is delayed from its early March launch window. NASA Administrator Jared Isaacman confirmed the delay Saturday after engineers found an interruption in helium flow during a 50-hour fueling test that used 730,000 gallons of propellant. This technical issue, involving helium for fuel tank pressurization and rocket cooling, will "almost assuredly impact the March window."

The Tri-State area is under its first blizzard warning since March 2017, with a powerful winter storm expected to deliver 12 to 18 inches of snow from Sunday afternoon through Monday morning. Snowfall rates could reach 2-3 inches per hour during the storm's peak, making travel extremely dangerous.

Mississippi health system shuts down clinics statewide after ransomware attack
The University of Mississippi Medical Center (UMMC) closed all 35 of its clinics statewide and canceled appointments, including chemotherapy and elective procedures, after a ransomware attack compromised its phone and electronic systems on Thursday. UMMC Vice Chancellor LouAnn Woodward stated Friday that the medical center is working with the FBI to resolve the system outage and is using paper documentation for ongoing patient care. UMMC hospitals and emergency departments remain operational.

Trump says he will increase his new global tariffs to 15%
Donald Trump announced on Truth Social Saturday that he will impose 15% global tariffs, the maximum allowed under a never-used 1974 trade law that permits them for about five months without congressional approval. This follows a 6-3 Supreme Court decision Friday, which struck down his previous import taxes under a 1977 law, finding he had overstepped his powers. The previous tariffs had collected at least $130 billion for the US.
