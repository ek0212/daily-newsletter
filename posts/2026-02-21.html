LLM-Generated Passwords Expose Major Security Flaws with Predictability, Repetition, and Weakness
New research reveals that passwords generated by LLMs like GPT, Claude, and Gemini are significantly weaker than they appear due to predictable patterns, not true randomness. For example, Claude Opus 4.6 produced only 30 unique passwords in 50 runs, with one repeating 18 times (36% probability), showing only an estimated 27 bits of entropy compared to the expected 98 bits for a strong 16-character password. GPT-5.2's 20-character passwords had roughly 20 bits of entropy, making them crackable in seconds, and both GPT-5.2 and Gemini 3 Flash showed strong biases in starting characters.
